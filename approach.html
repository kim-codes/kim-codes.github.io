How I
Approach My
First 90 Days.

A roadmap for how I'd spend my first year earning trust, creating momentum, and helping MongoDB tell better technical stories.

My philosophy

I don't believe great product marketing starts with messaging. It starts with understanding engineers, customers, and the problems they're trying to solve. Only then can we tell stories that resonate.
PHASE 01
FIRST 30 DAYS

Learn & Listen

Before trying to influence strategy, I want to understand the team, the product, and the customers.

  • Understand the company's platform, roadmap, GTM positioning, and developer ecosystem.
  • Build relationships across Sales, Product, Marketing, Customer Success.
  • Review launches, messaging, competitive positioning, and existing enablement content.
  • Identify quick wins without changing things just for the sake of change
PHASE 02
FIRST 60 DAYS

Build Credibility

Start contributing meaningful work while learning and building relationships & trust across teams.

  • Create technical content that helps customers understand product value.
  • Partner closely with PMs to translate technical features into compelling stories.
  • Contribute to launch planning and competitive intelligence.
  • Become someone teammates know they can rely on.
PRIMARY GOAL
PHASE 03
FIRST 90 DAYS

Take Ownership

By this point I want to move beyond more learning, and be contributing as much as I am learning.

  • Owning initiatives from planning through launch.
  • Develop technical messaging that resonates with devs and technical decision-makers.
  • Create scalable launch assets and technical enablement.
  • Be viewed as a trusted partner who brings customer insights into product conversations.
PHASE 04
BEYOND 90 DAYS | FIRST YEAR

Impact

Success isn't simply shipping launches—it's helping the entire organization make better product decisions.

  • Continuously improve messaging through experimentation, data, and customer conversations.
  • Create repeatable launch processes and technical content that scales.
  • Become the trusted bridge between engineering excellence and customer value.
  • Leave every launch, every team, and every customer conversation a little better than I found it.
BEYOND THE TIMELINE

What Success Looks Like

My goal isn't to have all the answers after one year. It's to become someone my teammates trust because I ask thoughtful questions, communicate clearly, and consistently follow through.

"The best product marketing isn't about having the loudest message. It's about helping customers understand why a product matters."
