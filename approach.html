How I'd Approach My First 90 Days

Every company is different. This isn't a checklist but a mindset. What I could I bring whenever I join a new team.

I don't believe in arriving with all the answers. Ask questions, staying curious, and have fun.
30 Days
Learn
& Listen

My first priority would be understanding how the product creates value for customers. This starts with documentation and learning channels, but also by listening. I'd spend time with fellow PMMs, PMs, Customer Success, and Sales to understand how each team talks about the product and where those perspectives overlap.

  • Understand the product, roadmap, and competitive landscape.
  • Build relationships across Sales, Product, Marketing, and Customer Success.
  • Listen to customer conversations and product demos.
  • Review existing messaging, launches, and technical content.
  • Learn why things work before suggesting how they could change.
60 Days
Contribute

By this point I'd want to be contributing in ways that make life easier for the people around me. Whether that's helping shape messaging, supporting a launch, or creating technical content, my goal would be to start adding value while continuing to learn. By this point, I'd want people to stop thinking of me as "the new PMM". Not because I've learned everything, but because I'm contributing and becoming a dependable teammate.

  • Support product launches and messaging initiatives.
  • Create content that helps technical audiences understand product value.
  • Look for opportunities to simplify complex ideas.
  • Become someone teammates know they can rely on.
90 Days
Take
Ownership

I'd still expect to be learning every day, but by this point I'd hope to have found my rhythm. I'd want to understand how things operate, know where I can add value, and feel confident taking on bigger challenges. Ideally, teammates are comfortable pulling me into hard problems because they know I'll figure them out

  • Lead a product marketing initiative from planning through launch.
  • Develop messaging that resonates with developers and technical decision-makers.
  • Bring customer insights back to Product.
  • Create scalable launch assets and technical enablement.
  • Become a trusted collaborator across Product, Engineering, and Go-to-Market teams.
Looking Ahead
Year One

Hopefully, nobody thinks I'm new anymore.

Success isn't just shipping launches. It's helping people understand the product, making thoughtful decisions, and leaving the team a little better than I found it. If I've helped tell clearer product stories, brought useful customer insights into conversations, and become someone people enjoy working with, I'd consider that a successful first year.

I don't expect to know everything after 90 days. I do expect to care enough to keep learning. • 2026
