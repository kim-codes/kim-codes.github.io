How I'd Approach My First 90 Days

Every company is different. This isn't a checklist but a mindset. What I could I bring whenever I join a new team.

I don't believe in arriving with all the answers. Ask questions, staying curious, and have fun.

First 30 Days | Learn & Listen

My first priority would be understanding how the product creates value for customers. This start with documentation and learning channels, but also by listening. I'd like to spend time with fellow PMMs, PMs, Customer Success, and Sales, to understand how each team talks about the product and where those perspectives overlap.

  • Understand the product, roadmap, and competitive landscape.
  • Build relationships across Sales, Product, Marketing, Customer Success.
  • Listen to customer conversations and product demos.
  • Review existing messaging, launches, and technical content.
  • Learn why things work before suggesting how they could change.

First 60 Days | Contribute

By this point, I'd want to be contributing in ways that make life easier for the people around me. Whether that's helping shape messaging, supporting a launch, or creating technical content, my goal would be start contributing more, but still a lot of learning.

  • Support product launches and messaging initiatives.
  • Create content that helps technical audiences understand product value.
  • Look for opportunities to simplify complex ideas.
  • Become someone teammates know they can rely on.

First 90 Days | Take Ownership

This is the milestone that matters most to me. If everything has gone well, I'd hope someone feels comfortable saying, "Can Kim own this?"

  • Lead a product marketing initiative from planning through launch.
  • Develop messaging that resonates with devs and technical decision-makers.
  • Bring customer insights back to Product.
  • Create scalable launch assets and technical enablement.
  • Become a trusted collaborator across Product, Engineering, and Go-to-Market teams.

Looking Ahead

After my first year, I don't expect to know everything. I hope to be someone teammates trust because I ask thoughtful questions, communicate clearly, and consistently follow through.

Success isn't simply shipping launches, but also about helping the entire organization make better product decisions. If I've helped launch products customers understand, strengthened collaboration across teams, and made someone's job a little easier along the way, I'd consider that a successful first year.
