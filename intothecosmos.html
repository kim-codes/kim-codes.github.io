Behind the Work

Making a Complex Product Memorable

How do you make a technical product memorable without making it feel less technical? Sometimes the answer isn't simplifying the technology. It's finding a story people want to follow.

Skills in Motion

Positioning & Messaging Sales Enablement Product Education Technical Storytelling Community Building Program Design Adoption Strategy Cross-functional Influence

Mission 01

The Mission

Building Field Confidence for a Complex Cloud Database Product

Mission 02

The Challenge

Field teams needed a lightweight way to understand when and why to position a distributed database product. The product was technically powerful, but broad field understanding was uneven because sellers and technical teams have many competing priorities.

Mission 03

The Blueprint

The product name had "Cosmos" in it, so the space theme felt like a natural creative hook. I wanted to give people something memorable to latch onto, not just another training deck. Inspired a bit by Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey. I didn't know it would resonate. But I knew people were tired of dry enablement, and giving them something with personality felt like a risk worth taking.

Mission 04

The Launch

A bite-sized internal learning series with recurring episodes, simple technical explainers, customer-use-case storytelling, myth-busting, and a champion-style recognition model.

Mission 05

The Legacy

The audience grew. Recurring cadence created anticipation, similar to a show. People were asking when the next episode was coming.
People wanted to contribute. Teams asked for follow-up content and deeper technical episodes. Others wanted to participate in epiosodes. Champions grew organically.
what surprised me? How much a fun theme lowered the barrier to engaging with a complex topic.

"The goal wasn't to make the technology simpler. It was to make people excited enough to explore it."
